Check theme-assignment quality

In Assign themes, the AI mapped every response to the themes your team finalised. Check quality is where you find out how well it did.

This step is about confidence. You want evidence that the assigning holds up — the analysis is only as sound as the assigning underneath it.

The AI did the assigning. Checking it is how you stand behind the findings.

How assignment quality is measured

There are two methods. Every consultation uses real time evaluation. It's built in and always runs. You can add prospective evaluation when you need a more rigorous, in-depth benchmark. Agree your approach with your central assurance team before you start.

A

Real-time assignment evaluation

This is the baseline for every consultation.

Your policy team reviews the AI's theme assignments in the analysis dashboard during Analyse phase, and corrects any that don't fit. Consult tracks how often you make corrections and where they cluster:

  • A low correction rate means the assignment is more reliable
  • Clusters of corrections point to areas worth a closer look.

You can see this reporting in Consult.

Real-time assignment evaluation works because your team's own corrections are the benchmark. You're not taking the assignment on trust — you're measuring it against what real reviewers decided.

To correct a response's themes

You do this as a normal part of reviewing in Analyse phase. When the themes on a response don't fit:

  • Click the edit icon on the response to see the themes it's been assigned.
  • Click the cross to remove any theme that doesn't belong.
  • Search the list to add any theme that's missing.
  • Click Save.

Your edit doesn't change what the AI assigned in Assign themes — that record is what we measure quality against. It changes how this response is labelled from now on, for you and for anyone else on your team who views it.

B

Prospective assignment evaluation

This is a deeper assessment, for when you need a more rigorous benchmark.

Two people map the same responses to themes, working independently. We compare their results to each other, then compare Consult's mapping to that human benchmark. This tells you something real-time evaluation can't: how much two trained people naturally disagree, and whether Consult performs within that range.

We ran this during Consult's first evaluations. You can read the results at:

  • DWP’s Pathways to Work consultation - August 2025
  • Independent Water Commission Call for Evidence - April 2025
  • Scottish Government’s Non-surgical cosmetic procedures consultation - March 2025

Before you start

We'll send you a link to the task. Decide as a team how to share the work: one person can evaluate the whole sample, or you can divide it between several people.

Evaluate each response

  • Read the response in full, alongside is the list of themes it could be assigned to
  • Tick the checkbox for each theme the response should be assigned to.
  • Save, and move to the next response.
  • Repeat until you've finished your sample

The main screen shows progress for each question, so you can see what's done and what's left.

Each response takes an average [x] seconds to evaluate. This varies with how well you know the question area and how long and complex the response is.

What happens after checking quality

If you did real-time evaluation: we'll send you the link to the analysis dashboard to start Analyse. We'll share the outcomes and discuss whether any action is needed.

If you did prospective evaluation: once you and your assurance team are satisfied with the assignment quality, we'll send you the link to the analysis dashboard to start Analyse. We'll also send the performance scoring to your Consultation or Departmental Owner, along with any issues that came up, so you can agree any action with us.
