Analyse

This is the final step. The AI has assigned your finalised themes to every response. Now you explore the data and build the evidence base for your consultation response.

The dashboard does not write your response or tell you what the policy should be. That is your job. What it gives you is a structured way to explore what respondents said, so you can write your response with confidence.


To analyse your consultation it helps to work in a cycle:

  • understand what the themes are telling you
  • read the responses
  • ask questions of the data
  • reflect on what you find to inform your policy conversations

You repeat this for each question or theme you analyse. Record your decisions and reasoning so you can evidence your conclusions and have an audit trail if needed.

The AI suggests, you decide. The dashboard gives you the evidence base. Working out what respondents said, and what it means for your policy, is your job.

The home page

The home page shows:

  • key metrics such as the number of questions and responses
  • demographic insights about your respondents
  • your consultation questions and their responses

Click the star to favourite key questions. They appear in the Favourited questions section, which makes them easier to find.

Step 1: Open a question

Select a question to open its analysis. Everything for that question sits on a single page:

  • the multiple choice answers shown as a graph at the top
  • filters for any demographic questions you collected, to the left
  • the list of themes in the centre of the page
  • all the responses for that question, below the list of themes

Step 2: Review the themes

For each theme, the theme list shows its:

  • title
  • description
  • number of mentions
  • percentage of mentions

This lets you understand the most common themes, and the descriptions give key insights about the responses. Click Export to download this data.

Here we are reviewing the list of themes to understand the insights they give about the responses.

Step 3: Filter to explore patterns

You can use the multiple choice graph and the demographic questions to filter the list of themes and the individual responses, so you can see how these change for different groups.

On the multiple choice graph, click Support and Oppose to see how this changes the themes, insights and proportions.

On the demographic questions, filter by:

  • location, to see whether people in different regions raised different concerns
  • respondent type, to compare individuals and organisations

What you can filter by depends on the demographic questions you included in your survey and how you designed your closed questions.

Here we are filtering by the multiple choice results Support and Oppose to see how this changes the themes, insights and proportions, and by respondent type to compare individuals and organisations.

Step 4: Read the individual responses

Build a deeper understanding of your themes by reading the individual responses. To do this:

  • click a theme to select it
  • scroll to see the response cards for that theme, tagged for type, location and opinion

A single response can have more than one theme, for example if someone makes several points.

Here we are clicking on a theme and reading the individual responses.

Step 5: Find evidence-rich responses

Below the search box is a row of toggles. Turn on Evidence rich to show only the responses labelled as evidence-rich. These may give a better indication of why someone holds their view, or include facts or evidence to back up their reasoning.

The Unread toggle shows the responses you haven't read yet, which helps you keep track of what you've been through.

Here we are filtering by Evidence rich to find responses that give a better indication of why someone holds their view, or include facts or evidence to back up their reasoning.

Step 6: Search for key terms

You may think a theme has not been included. You can't add new themes, but you can use the search box at the top of the response list to search for common keywords.

Step 7: Flag important responses

Each response card has a flag icon. Click it to flag responses that are helpful, that you want to discuss as a team or that you might want to quote in your report.

The Flagged toggle at the top of the responses shows only the responses you've flagged. This makes it easy to come back to the most useful ones when you write up.

Here we are marking an individual response with a flag, then using the Flagged toggle to find all the flagged responses.

8

Step 8: Edit a response's themes

If you think a response has been assigned to the wrong theme, you can make changes. To correct a response's theme:

  • click the edit icon on the response to see the themes it's been assigned
  • remove a theme by clicking the cross
  • add a theme by searching the list and clicking the +
  • click Save to save your edits — these then show a green Edited badge

The tool is flexible and you're not locked into the AI's decisions. Your policy expertise matters here. If you disagree with how something has been assigned, change it. Consult tracks the rate and patterns of these corrections.

Here we are clicking the edit icon to remove a theme and add a different theme, then clicking Save. The themes assigned to the response have changed, shown by the Edited badge.
